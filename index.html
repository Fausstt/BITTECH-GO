What Is Bitcoin Trading All About?

In a nutshell, Bitcoin trading is all about buying coins when the value is low and selling them when the value goes up. Say, for example, one Bitcoin is worth $40,000 (not an accurate valuation- only to be used as an example!). You buy 0.10 Bitcoin for $4000 at the beginning of the day.

Then, the value increases by two percent that day. Your purchase is now worth a little more, and you are up $80. It sounds small with these margins, but if you buy at the right time and hold until the perfect moment, it can total to a lot more. Remember, Bitcoin trading comes with no guarantees. Never invest more than you can afford, and don't get caught up in the moment. It is an exciting industry full of potential, but it is not a get-rich-quick scheme.

How do I get a digital wallet, and how does it work?

A digital wallet works exactly like a bank account, except for cryptocurrency and it is not controlled by an organization. When you set up one of these wallets, you are the only person who can gain access to it or view its contents. They come with two security keys: one for access and one for transaction confirmations.

You can choose between an online or offline digital wallet. Online wallets are set up via a crypto platform- usually a large-scale exchange. Be sure to choose one that supports all the currencies you are interested in buying. Offline wallets are physical hardware- usually in the style of a USB stick. Funds moved onto the sick are entirely secure- as long as you don’t lose it!

Buying an offline digital wallet is easy relativity. They are available online and in most technology - driven stores. Setting up an online wallet is even easier. It can even be done semi-anonymously, should that matter.
